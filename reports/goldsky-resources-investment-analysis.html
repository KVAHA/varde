Company Report April 2026

Goldsky Resources — Investment Analysis

Published April 13, 2026 · 20 min read
Executive Summary

First Nordic Metals is no longer just First Nordic Metals. In December 2025, following the acquisition of Mawson Finland, the company changed its name to Goldsky Resources and repositioned itself as a Nordic-focused gold and cobalt developer anchored by the Barsele gold project in Sweden and the Rajapalot gold-cobalt project in Finland. That matters because the investment case is no longer about one Swedish exploration platform. It is now about whether a broadened Nordic precious-metals portfolio creates real value or simply a larger narrative.

There is real substance here. Barsele remains a significant undeveloped Swedish gold project, where the 2019 resource estimate for the Avan, Central and Skiråsen zones showed 2.086 Moz inferred and 0.324 Moz indicated gold attributable at the project level, with First Nordic historically owning 45% through the joint venture with Agnico Eagle. Rajapalot adds a more advanced Finnish gold-cobalt development case, where the December 2023 PEA showed a post-tax NPV5 of US$211 million and IRR of 27%, based on an inferred resource of 9.78 Mt at 2.8 g/t gold and 441 ppm cobalt, containing 867 koz gold and 4,311 tonnes cobalt.

But this is still not a mine-builder in the way Viscaria is, nor a proven producer in the way Boliden is. It remains a pre-production developer/explorer whose valuation depends on advancing projects through resource growth, studies, permitting, and eventually financing. At 30 September 2025, before the Mawson transaction closed, First Nordic had C$9.85 million cash, C$22.25 million equity, and had recorded a C$22.4 million loss for the first nine months of 2025. That is enough to fund active exploration and corporate activity, not enough to independently build multiple mines.

My overall judgment is: this is a serious Nordic gold-development platform, but still mainly a portfolio of future possibilities rather than a clearly financeable near-term mining company. Investment stance: interesting strategic exposure to Nordic gold/cobalt, but still highly dependent on future de-risking and capital markets.

Investment Thesis

The positive case is that Goldsky now controls a better portfolio than the market may fully appreciate. Barsele gives the company exposure to a sizeable Swedish gold resource beside Agnico Eagle. Rajapalot adds a separate Finnish gold-cobalt project with a real PEA and a more direct development arc. Oijärvi and the wider Gold Line Belt add district-scale discovery optionality. This creates a more credible Nordic precious-metals platform than the old Barsele-only structure.

The negative case is that the portfolio is broader than the execution base. Investors are being asked to ascribe value across: a minority stake in a joint venture at Barsele; a separate Finnish PEA-stage asset at Rajapalot; and a large amount of exploration land with no guarantee of conversion into mineable ounces.

That structure can justify upside, but it can also obscure the fact that none of the core assets is yet a built mine or fully financed construction project. The company's own December 2025 presentation framed Goldsky as a "premier gold developer in the Nordics," but the practical next steps are still permitting, studies, project advancement, and disciplined capital deployment rather than near-term production.

So the real investment question is: does combining Barsele, Rajapalot, and the broader Nordic exploration portfolio create a coherent future producer, or mainly a larger and more marketable exploration narrative?

Company Overview

The company originally emerged from Barsele Minerals, then became First Nordic Metals after the Gold Line combination, and then became Goldsky Resources after acquiring Mawson Finland in December 2025. Today it should be understood as a Nordic gold and cobalt developer/explorer, with Sweden and Finland as its operating focus.

That corporate evolution matters because it changes how the company should be analyzed. This is no longer just a micro-cap Swedish explorer. It is a multi-asset Nordic development platform, with:

  • Barsele in Sweden as the flagship JV resource asset
  • Rajapalot in Finland as the main PEA-stage development asset
  • Oijärvi/Kylmäkangas in Finland as a broader greenstone-belt position
  • The Gold Line Belt in Sweden as district-scale exploration upside

Asset and Project Analysis

The strongest asset from a pure resource-scale perspective is still Barsele. The 2019 resource estimate cited in the company's annual reporting showed 25.495 Mt at 2.54 g/t Au for 2.086 Moz inferred, and 5.578 Mt at 1.81 g/t Au for 0.324 Moz indicated at the project level. Because First Nordic historically held 45%, the value to shareholders depends partly on whether the company can eventually consolidate more of the project or at least derive value through a JV outcome with Agnico Eagle.

Rajapalot is the more directly developmental asset. The December 2023 PEA showed a post-tax NPV5 of US$211 million and IRR of 27%, based on inferred resources only. That is a respectable starting point, but investors should remember the usual limitation: a PEA based on inferred resources is still preliminary and far from financing-grade. The project is attractive because it combines gold and cobalt in Finland, not because it is already construction-ready.

Oijärvi and the broader Gold Line Belt are where much of the optionality lives. These areas may eventually add value through new discoveries, but for now they should be treated as upside rather than as core valuation anchors.

My judgment is that the asset base is meaningfully stronger after the Mawson combination, but value remains concentrated in a few key projects and still largely ahead of the drill bit, study cycle, and permitting curve.

Operating and Technical Assessment

This is still a non-producing company. So the technical question is not operational uptime or mill performance. It is whether the company is advancing its projects in a disciplined sequence.

On that front, the company has made progress. The Q3 2025 report highlighted a fully oversubscribed C$15.4 million LIFE financing closed in July 2025 and early assays from the Aida drill program. The December 2025 Goldsky deck then reframed the company around clearer flagship assets and permitting/advancement milestones, especially for Rajapalot.

But technically, the company still sits in a portfolio-de-risking phase, not in an engineering-delivery phase. Barsele remains resource-led and JV-dependent. Rajapalot has a PEA but still needs permitting and additional de-risking. The exploration properties are earlier still. So while the technical story is credible, it is not yet close to bankable project execution.

Commodity and Market Context

The company benefits from two supportive thematic exposures: gold, which provides monetary/defensive appeal and can support equity valuations in uncertain macro environments; and cobalt, which adds strategic and energy-transition relevance at Rajapalot.

This is an interesting mix. It is not as obvious a "critical minerals" story as a rare earth or graphite company, but Rajapalot's cobalt content does give the group some strategic relevance beyond ordinary gold exploration. The company's own presentation explicitly frames Rajapalot as aligned with EU strategic and energy-transition priorities.

That said, gold-development equities are still governed mainly by familiar rules: resource quality, study credibility, permitting, financing, and ultimately whether ounces can be mined at acceptable returns. Sector context helps this company. It does not replace de-risking.

Management, Ownership, and Governance

Management has pursued an aggressive consolidation strategy. First Nordic's transformation from Barsele-only exposure into a broader Nordic platform, and then into Goldsky after Mawson, shows ambition and capital-markets skill. The company's Q3 2025 capital raise and successful transaction execution also suggest that management has been able to tell a story the market was willing to fund.

There is also some external validation in the shareholder and project structure. Agnico Eagle remains central through Barsele and received shares in connection with the Oijärvi acquisition, reinforcing that major counterparties are not absent from the story.

But governance should be judged by whether management now narrows the focus and advances the best assets, or continues adding optionality faster than value is crystallized. In a company like this, strategic breadth can become a strength or a distraction.

Financial Review

Financially, this remains a development-stage equity. At 30 September 2025, the company reported C$9.85 million cash, C$27.2 million total assets, C$22.25 million shareholders' equity, and a C$22.41 million loss for the first nine months of 2025.

Those numbers matter because they confirm the obvious: the company is not funding its path from operations. It is funding it from the capital markets. That is normal for a company at this stage, but it means valuation should be treated as expectation-based, not earnings-based.

The December 2025 Goldsky presentation said the combined company would be fully funded with C$80M+ treasury after the Mawson transaction. Even if that is directionally true, it still funds project advancement and exploration, not multiple mine builds. Investors should not mistake a healthy exploration treasury for construction financing.

Capital Structure and Financing Risk

Financing risk is not acute in the near term in the same way it is for a nearly-built project company, because Goldsky is not yet trying to close a full mine-construction package. The immediate question is treasury runway and capital efficiency. On that front, the company is in a better position than many explorers thanks to successful raisings and the combined post-merger treasury.

But longer term, financing risk is still central. The company's current assets are mostly exploration and evaluation assets, a JV interest, and early-stage development assets. If Rajapalot or a future Barsele consolidation moves toward development, the capital needs will rise sharply.

So the capital-structure conclusion is: Goldsky is funded to explore and advance. It is not yet funded to build.

Jurisdiction and Permitting Analysis

This is one of the strongest parts of the case. Sweden and Finland are among the better mining jurisdictions globally in terms of institutional quality, infrastructure, and mining tradition. Goldsky's own materials lean heavily on this, citing Finland and Sweden as tier-one jurisdictions and emphasizing local teams, ESG-first positioning, and permitting progress at Rajapalot.

That is a real advantage. In a development-stage portfolio company, jurisdiction quality matters because it raises the probability that a good asset can eventually become a mine. It does not eliminate risk, but it makes the platform more serious than a similar basket of projects in weaker jurisdictions.

Strategic Positioning and Peer Context

Strategically, Goldsky occupies an unusual spot in the Nordic universe. It is earlier than Boliden, less financeable today than Viscaria, less single-project than most juniors, and more advanced than pure grassroots explorers. That makes it hard to value cleanly, but also potentially interesting.

If management can prove that Rajapalot is a real development asset and that Barsele can be monetized or consolidated intelligently, Goldsky could evolve into a genuine Nordic developer rather than just an exploration roll-up. The risk is that the market gives it credit for being a future producer before the projects are advanced enough to justify that.

Key Risks and Dependencies

The biggest risk is portfolio diffusion risk. There are enough projects here for investors to imagine large upside, but also enough moving parts for management attention and capital to be spread thin.

The second risk is JV and ownership-structure risk at Barsele. First Nordic historically held 45%, not 100%, which means value realization depends partly on a project structure the company does not fully control.

The third risk is study and permitting risk at Rajapalot. A 2023 PEA is useful, but still far from financing-grade, and the project remains dependent on future permitting and additional technical de-risking.

The fourth risk is capital-markets dependency. However strong the geological story, this remains a company funded by investor support rather than mine cash flow.

Valuation

Valuation here is inherently imprecise, because the company is a portfolio of partially de-risked assets rather than a producing business or a single financeable mine.

The company's own December 2025 presentation showed FNM (pre-transaction) at roughly C$154 million market cap / C$149 million EV, and Goldsky (pre-consolidation) at around C$342 million market cap / C$255 million EV based on management's framing of the combined entity.

That tells you something important: the market is already paying a meaningful premium for the broader platform. Whether that is justified depends on whether Rajapalot and Barsele move meaningfully closer to development and whether exploration success continues.

My valuation view is:

  • Base case: interesting but not obviously cheap; the market is already paying for a multi-asset development platform.
  • Upside case: strong if Rajapalot advances cleanly, Barsele value is crystallized, and exploration success continues.
  • Downside case: material if the platform remains broad but under-advanced, forcing repeated capital raises without a clear path to mine development.

Scenario Analysis

In the base case, Goldsky continues to advance Rajapalot through permitting and studies, keeps Barsele strategically relevant, and uses its treasury to drill and refine the portfolio without forcing near-term distress financing. That supports the current valuation, but not necessarily a dramatic rerating.

In the upside case, Rajapalot becomes a more clearly financeable Finnish mine-development project, Barsele is revalued or consolidated on favorable terms, and the market begins to see the company less as an explorer and more as the leading Nordic gold-development platform management wants to build.

In the downside case, the company remains a story of promising projects and corporate activity, but without enough project-level conversion into mineable, financeable assets. In that world, valuation support erodes.

Catalysts and What to Watch

The most important catalyst is Rajapalot advancement, especially permitting and any updated study work that moves the project beyond the existing 2023 PEA. That is the clearest route from narrative to development value.

The second is Barsele strategy. Investors should watch whether management can improve the company's position there, either through resource growth, clearer JV value recognition, or a path toward greater control.

The third is exploration efficiency. Goldsky has enough land and targets to keep announcing results; what matters is whether those results improve the economic center of gravity of the company, not just the size of the story.

The fourth is capital discipline. With a broader portfolio and treasury, the market should expect prioritization, not just activity.

Conclusion

First Nordic Metals, now Goldsky Resources, is one of the more interesting Nordic precious-metals platform stories because it sits between pure exploration and true development. There is enough here to take seriously: Barsele is a real resource asset, Rajapalot is a real PEA-stage project, and the jurisdictional setting is strong.

But the company is still better described as a promising Nordic development platform than as an investable future producer with a clear near-term build path.

My final judgment is: Goldsky has real asset quality and strategic merit, but the equity still depends on future conversion of portfolio promise into project-level certainty. Investment view: interesting, but still speculative and highly dependent on de-risking discipline.

The company deserves attention. It does not yet deserve unquestioning valuation confidence.
